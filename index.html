Pack-by-LED

Streamlining shipping operations

Pack-by-LED is a fast, visual way to guide warehouse operators during the packing process. LEDs show what to pack next: simple and very efficient.

The Challenge

In the warehouse, picking is done across multiple zones. Before shipping, all items must be consolidated into one shipment. At the same time, there are different cut-off and shipping times depending on the customer, destination, and carrier.

Operators had to check small screens on handheld scanners or PCs to see which shipments were complete and ready for packing. This resulted in manually searching through racks before starting to pack — a time-consuming and error-prone process.

The Solution

With Pack-by-LED, operators no longer need to check any screens. When all items for a shipment are present in the rack, the LEDs light up automatically.

The LED color indicates the cut-off time or shipping priority:

Orange – early cut-off shipment
Green – standard shipment
Blue – late cut-off shipment

This allows operators to instantly see which shipments are ready and what to prioritize: no searching, no delays.

From Pick-by-Light to Pack-by-LED

Traditional pick-by-light systems could have been an option. We initially explored that approach but found the buttons and screens unnecessary. During the packing process, operators already use a PDA or handheld scanner, so adding buttons only makes things slower and more expensive.

Pack-by-LED eliminates the button completely. The LEDs are controlled directly by the software, triggered by scan events or shipment status. This makes the system faster, cheaper, easier to install, and much more flexible than traditional pick-by-light hardware.

Key Benefits

  • Speed: Immediate LED feedback keeps operators moving efficiently.
  • Accuracy: Each color represents a shipment priority. Updated in instantly.
  • Ease of use: Intuitive and easy to learn. No training required.
  • Scalability: More racks? Simply add LED strips and Wi-Fi controllers.
  • Plug & Play: Quick to install, no special infrastructure needed.
  • Future-proof: Only standard components are used.

How it works

  • Uses addressable LED strips connected to small Wi-Fi controllers.
  • Controlled by a lightweight software service running on a server (or local device).
  • Connects to database (or API, CSV files,...) from WMS (or ERP) system.
  • LEDs update in real time based on shipment progress.
  • Hardware is low-cost, modular, and easy to expand.
